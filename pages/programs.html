Flagship Program

Gi BJJ

Ground grappling performed while wearing the traditional training garment known as the gi, or kimono. The heavy cotton uniform simulates everyday clothing, and practitioners emphasize gi manipulation for controlling opponents. Both gi and no-gi classes are included in BJJ memberships.

Learn More →
No-Gi Grappling

No-Gi BJJ

Ground grappling performed without the traditional uniform. Athletes wear shorts, tights, and rash guards. The manipulation of an opponent's clothing is prohibited, making control techniques more demanding than gi training. Included in BJJ memberships.

Learn More →
Striking

Muay Thai

Thai boxing — the art of eight limbs — using fists, elbows, knees, and shins. Classes include warm-ups, technique instruction, bag and pad work, shadow boxing, drills, and conditioning. Shorts, a t-shirt, and boxing gloves are required.

Kids Classes
