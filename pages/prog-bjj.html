The foundation of everything we do. Whether you're day one white belt or a seasoned competitor, BJJ at Grant's is structured, technical, and ego-free.

What to Expect

Our BJJ program runs classes six days a week across all skill levels. Fundamentals classes focus on core positions, escapes, and submissions — taught in a structured, drill-heavy format designed so beginners thrive and experienced practitioners sharpen their foundations.

Advanced classes assume a working knowledge of the basics and cover competition strategies, advanced guard systems, and live positional sparring. Open mat sessions give you unstructured time to flow with training partners at your own pace.

Belt promotions are earned — not bought. Progress is recognised when you're ready, guided by our black belt coaching staff led by Alex MacDonald and TJ Grant.

Belt Curriculum

Your Path to Black Belt

White Belt

Foundations · 0–12 months
  • Positional control (mount, guard, side control)
  • Basic escapes: bridge-and-roll, elbow-knee
  • Rear naked choke, triangle, armbar
  • Guard passing fundamentals
  • Takedown defense

Blue Belt

Development · 1–2 years
  • Open guard variations (De La Riva, spider)
  • Guard sweeps and combinations
  • Submission chains and setups
  • Positional dominance concepts
  • Drilling sequences

Purple Belt

Refinement · 2–4 years
  • Advanced guard systems
  • Leg lock entries and defense
  • Competition strategy
  • Troubleshooting personal game
  • Beginning to teach fundamentals

Brown / Black Belt

Mastery · 4+ years
  • Complete, well-rounded game
  • High-level competition or coaching track
  • Unique style and game development
  • Understanding of meta and principles
  • Leadership within the community
