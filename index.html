Gi BJJ

Ground grappling performed while wearing the traditional training garment known as the gi, or kimono. The heavy cotton uniform simulates everyday clothing, and practitioners emphasize gi manipulation for controlling opponents. Both gi and no-gi classes are included in BJJ memberships.

Ground grappling performed without the traditional uniform. Athletes wear shorts, tights, and rash guards. The manipulation of an opponent's clothing is prohibited, making control techniques more demanding than gi training. Included in BJJ memberships.

Jiu Jitsu

The foundation of everything we do. Whether you're day one white belt or a seasoned competitor, BJJ at Grant's is structured, technical, and ego-free.

What to Expect

Our BJJ program runs classes six days a week across all skill levels. Fundamentals classes focus on core positions, escapes, and submissions — taught in a structured, drill-heavy format designed so beginners thrive and experienced practitioners sharpen their foundations.

Advanced classes assume a working knowledge of the basics and cover competition strategies, advanced guard systems, and live positional sparring. Open mat sessions give you unstructured time to flow with training partners at your own pace.

Belt promotions are earned — not bought. Progress is recognised when you're ready, guided by our black belt coaching staff led by Alex MacDonald and TJ Grant.

Belt Curriculum

Your Path to Black Belt

White Belt

Foundations · 0–12 months
  • Positional control (mount, guard, side control)
  • Basic escapes: bridge-and-roll, elbow-knee
  • Rear naked choke, triangle, armbar
  • Guard passing fundamentals
  • Takedown defense

Blue Belt

Development · 1–2 years
  • Open guard variations (De La Riva, spider)
  • Guard sweeps and combinations
  • Submission chains and setups
  • Positional dominance concepts
  • Drilling sequences

Purple Belt

Refinement · 2–4 years
  • Advanced guard systems
  • Leg lock entries and defense
  • Competition strategy
  • Troubleshooting personal game
  • Beginning to teach fundamentals

Brown / Black Belt

Mastery · 4+ years
  • Complete, well-rounded game
  • High-level competition or coaching track
  • Unique style and game development
  • Understanding of meta and principles
  • Leadership within the community

No-Gi

Submission wrestling without the gi. Heel hooks, leg locks, and tight scrambles — the cutting edge of modern grappling taught by black belts who compete in it.

What to Expect

American Jiu Jitsu (AJJ) and No-Gi grappling at Grant's covers the full modern submission wrestling ruleset — including leg entanglements, heel hooks, and the reaping positions that are central to today's competition meta.

Classes focus on building sequences from position, understanding when to attack vs. defend, and developing the mechanical clarity to apply technique under pressure. Jacob Moses teaches Friday evenings with an emphasis on creative guard play and passing chains.

Recommended for athletes with at least some grappling experience. Blue belt or equivalent background suggested.

The Training Surface

World-Class Mats.
Built to Last.

Our mats aren't an afterthought — they're the single most important investment in the gym. We use professional-grade tatami-surface jigsaw puzzle mats with a 40mm compressed EVA foam core, the same standard used at IBJJF World Championships and UFC training facilities.

The tatami weave on the surface provides exactly the right amount of grip — enough to hold position through scrambles, not so much that it tears skin on stand-ups. Underneath, the high-density foam core absorbs impact correctly: it compresses under dynamic load (throws, takedowns, sprawls) but doesn't bottom out. That combination protects joints, reduces cumulative fatigue, and means you can train more days in a row without your knees paying for it.

The foam is closed-cell, which matters more than most people realize. Closed-cell EVA doesn't absorb sweat or bacteria into the core — it stays at the surface where it can be properly disinfected. We clean the mats after every session with hospital-grade antimicrobial solution. The mats you train on here will outlast you at this gym.

Why Mat Quality Matters

Cheap mats are a false economy. Thin foam bottoms out — your knees hit the concrete subfloor on every takedown. Slippery surfaces spike injury rates on stand-ups and scrambles. Porous foam traps bacteria and is impossible to fully sanitize, which is how skin infections spread through gyms. We've seen what happens at facilities that cut corners here and we made a different choice from day one.

Good mats let you train longer careers. They're an investment in every person who steps on them.
